WALKER FLYNN

I’ve always been fascinated by making things. I learned how to blow glass when I was eleven, I started welding at thirteen. When I was eighteen I wanted to be an artist. I applied to art schools and left California to study at RISD. There I majored in furniture design. During this time I was exposed to CAD, which I took to instantly. As I continued to explore the potentials of CAD and digital fabrication I came across grasshopper, a procedural modelling plug in for Rhino. This was my first exposure to the basic concepts of programming and I loved it. After school I went to work at a design and mechanical engineering consultancy called Inch. Inch held “work beautifully” as it’s motto. While there I learned to make products that worked beautifully, both as an experience and a mechanism. Inch was a really small team, about five people, within my first few months I was leading projects and presenting to clients. After working at Inch for a couple years it became clear to me that the really interesting features, the stuff I wanted to make, were on the software side. I already knew that programming was something I enjoyed, but I needed to get better and fast. I applied to a number of programming intensives, decided on Hack Reactor. I've been working as software developer since completing the program in 2013.
